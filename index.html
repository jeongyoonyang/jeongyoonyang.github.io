Jeongyoon Yang

Hello! I am Jeongyoon Yang, a first-year Master’s student in the Department of Communication at Yonsei University.

My research interests center on polarization in academic knowledge production and the role of inclusive research practices in fostering understanding across divided groups.

Multicultural Identity and Narrative

My research begins with an exploration of multicultural identity, particularly how individuals navigate diverse cultural backgrounds to form a cohesive sense of self. Having experienced education across different cultural landscapes, I am interested in how these hybrid identities are articulated and represented. My goal is to examine the narratives that emerge at the intersection of various cultures and how they contribute to a broader understanding of global citizenship.

Social Interaction in Digital Media

I investigate the evolving nature of human interaction within digital media environments. As our daily lives become increasingly inseparable from smartphone screens and social platforms, the way we connect with others is undergoing a fundamental shift. I explore how media selection and consumption patterns influence social bonds, seeking to identify the psychological mechanisms that drive our engagement with digital content and each other.

Polarization and Inclusive Research Practices

A primary focus of my work is addressing the growing problem of polarization in academic and social discourse. I study how fragmented groups can find common ground through inclusive research practices that prioritize empathy and mutual understanding. By fostering cross-group dialogue and integrating diverse perspectives, my research seeks to dismantle echo chambers and lay the foundation for a more inclusive and harmonious intellectual environment.
